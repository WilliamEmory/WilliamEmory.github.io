  • How My Classes Have Prepared Me for a Career in Problem Solving ▼

    My GE classes at Iowa State have taught more than simply how to code. While it is true Iowa State does a great job of teaching C and Java, those languages comprise only a small subset of what I know. I have learned JavaScript, Python, C++, SQL, basic Android development, how to create a website, how to extract information from a Hadoop server cluster, embedded systems, and more. Iowa State taught me how to teach myself. Many classes do not offer simple answers to problems, and as a student it is my job to use anything at my disposal (languages, data structures, etc.) to solve a problem.

    An example of a general elective course offering me insight into an engineering problem was my English 314 class. It focused on various forms of visual media. As a result I learned some key basics of design for brochures, websites, memos, and other forms of communication. There is more to being an engineer then simply coding. Making a well-designed website is just as important as making a well working one. Iowa State did a great job of making me well rounded, so I can function in the business world, not just function in front of a computer.
